01 — Executive Summary

The Big Picture

VoltFleet is a Las Vegas–based car rental business targeting the city's 40+ million annual tourists. We start lean — a small fleet of reliable used Toyotas, Hondas, and a Ford Mustang sourced at auction — and scale to a direct booking brand serving tourists, corporate clients, and eventually premium and EV tiers as the business grows.

Las Vegas is one of the highest-demand car rental markets in the country. Most visitors fly in and need wheels. The traditional rental market is dominated by Hertz, Enterprise, and Avis — slow, expensive, and boring. Turo gives us access to that same demand with a fraction of the overhead. Our unfair advantage: a hands-on airplane mechanic who understands mechanical systems at a deep level, paired with a business operator who can run listings, pricing, and partnerships. Low overhead. High leverage.

🔑 The Turo Fee Structure

Turo takes a platform fee from both sides. As a host, you keep 75–85% of the trip price depending on your protection plan. With the Turo-provided insurance plan (easiest for starting out), you keep ~75%. On a $65/day rental, you net ~$49. That's your baseline. As you grow and qualify as an All-Star Host, Turo boosts your listing visibility — compounding the revenue effect.

💰 Acquisition Strategy

Auction-only. Repossessed vehicles with clean title — no salvage, no rebuilt. Justin brings an OBD2 scanner to every auction, walks the lot, checks the engine, and only bids on what he'd stake his name on. Target under 130K miles and 2014 or newer to stay within Turo's listing requirements. Starting fleet: 3–5 vehicles. Buy the same models where possible — same oil, same parts, one supplier relationship, dramatically lower per-unit service cost.

EV as Tier 2+: Once Phase 1 fleet is cash flow positive for a full quarter, evaluate adding an EV tier. Las Vegas has the charging infrastructure. Premium pricing, aspirational listings, separate segment. We get there by earning it first.

📈 Upside Scenario

Vegas has massive event-driven pricing windows. SEMA (November), CES (January), EDC (May), Formula 1 (November), New Year's Eve — rates during these periods can 2–3x. A Mustang at $150–200/day during sold-out weekends changes the math entirely. At 20 days/month with event pricing mixed in, a 3-car fleet can generate $3,500–5,000+ net. The annual P&L changes dramatically when you own the event calendar.

08 — Go-to-Market

How We Get Bookings

Phase 1: Win Turo

The Turo algorithm rewards reviews, response rate, and listing quality. Day one tasks: hire a professional photographer for car photos (makes a measurable difference), write detailed descriptions with Las Vegas-specific value props ("perfect for the Strip," "charge overnight at the hotel"), enable instant book, and set competitive but premium pricing. Get to 10 reviews in the first 30 days by pricing slightly below market initially, then raise rates once reviews are established.

Phase 2: Build the Brand

🌐

VoltFleet.com

Simple direct booking site. Stripe payments. SEO-optimized for "car rental Las Vegas," "Mustang rental Las Vegas." Captures direct bookings at 100% margin (no Turo cut).

📸

Content / Social

Instagram + TikTok showing the cars in iconic LV locations. "Behind the scenes" fleet ops content. Anthony can tie this to existing content workflows.

🤝

Hotel Partnerships

Approach boutique hotels and Airbnb management companies about referral arrangements. "Our guests need a rental" is a warm lead. Revenue share on referrals.

Phase 3: Corporate Accounts

Target: casinos, real estate brokerages, law firms, convention companies, film productions. Offer monthly rates (10–15% below daily equivalent) with dedicated account service. Anthony leads outreach and contracts. One $3,000/month corporate account replaces 40+ individual Turo bookings in admin overhead.

10 — Risk Analysis

What Could Go Wrong + How We Handle It

Vehicle Damage / Accident
A renter damages a car, putting it out of commission and creating a cash crunch.
Mitigation Use Turo's premium host protection plan. Thorough pre/post-trip photos every time. Justin's inspection process catches latent damage before it escalates. Maintain a $5K cash reserve.
HIGH
Low Occupancy / Slow Season
Cars sit idle in January–February, killing cash flow when loan payments still hit.
Mitigation Dynamic pricing drops to fill slow days. Winter in Vegas still draws visitors (CES is January). Monthly locals rental option. Never drop below loan payment + carry costs.
MED
Turo Rule Changes / Platform Risk
Turo changes fees or policies, squeezing margins or blocking certain vehicles.
Mitigation Don't be 100% dependent on one platform. Add Getaround early. Build direct booking site by month 9. Corporate accounts remove platform dependency entirely.
MED
EV Depreciation
Used EV values have dropped significantly. Depreciation may outpace asset value.
Mitigation EVs are already depreciated. Buying used means the biggest drop already happened. ROI is calculated on the down payment + cash flow, not exit value. Plan is cash flow, not appreciation.
LOW
Co-Founder Conflict
Disagreements on spending, growth pace, or time investment damage the partnership.
Mitigation Write an operating agreement before launch. Define equity split (we suggest 50/50 with Anthony retaining 51% as managing member). Define what happens if one party wants out. Quarterly check-ins.
LOW
